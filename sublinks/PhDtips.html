Tools:

Backups:

Not only of biological samples but also of the software, the code etc. Keep at two different places or even three places. So in case of physical emergency like a fire, atleast one of them will be safe. Cloud back up such as onedrive ,dropbox can be a good option, but often it is at the mercy of the privacy policy of the provider. So either encrypt all the data or find your own cloud storage device. It is good to paranoid about contamination, so keep backups of your biological specimens at a safe and secure place.

Fiji:

If you work image processing. Learn to use FIJI. It can do everything that

Proprietary software can do and much more. Plus it is completely scritable and free.

Scripting:

Learn a scripting language. By this I dont mean Matlab . Matlab with its licenses is a nightmare for people outside of academia and often are extremely expensive. If you want to keep your code open and free. Use open source languages like python which can do almost everything matlab can do and much more. For those of you used to the

Statistics:

Learn to use R. R with Rstudio is easier and a pleasure to use. ggplot2 for plotting and any of the variety of the massive library at the disposal of R for your analysis. Take some time to make interactive plots in the lines of shiny or d3.js, other scientists will appreciate you for that.

Question:

The one question or several questions that will drive your research. It should be accepted that things don't always work out as intended. The question or the questions will change with time. It is important to have a question early into the PhD. If not at the beginning at least within a year or two. Else it is seriously important to evaluate the meaning of the PhD. Ideally these questions should emerge from your interests or your experiments. It is important to have at least some knowledge of the possible PhD topic. If not, atleast from the PI or the senior in the area of your research topic. Senior not in the years spent in in lab but rather senior in the thinking straightforward. Often these people are aware of the limitations of the subject or the field. In case you don't do wet lab will work, it is best to find a biologist who don't dismiss you outright or intimidated by you. Biology is never straight forward. That is why the existence of 4 million protocols trying to bring the degree of accuracy and reproducibility to the experiments.

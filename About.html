I am looking for research positions to investigate the mechanisms by which an organ is built from the bottom-up. For addressing such questions, I can employ a variety of techniques including experimental genetics, microscopy techniques, Image analysis, systems analysis and computational modelling.

I am doing my PhD in Basler lab in experimental developmental genetics to complement my engineering skill set. I have a Masters degree in Computational and systems biology from KTH Royal Institute of Technology in Stockholm and a bachelors in Electronics and Communication engineering. I worked for a few years as a software developer at Intergraph in the SG&I division.

During my masters in Stockholm, I took courses on molecular biology, oncology and worked on projects at Karolinska Institute and Oxford University for a better understanding of biological research. My master thesis at D-BSSE, ETH in the lab of Dagmar Iber is modelling the digit patterning during limb development (published paper).

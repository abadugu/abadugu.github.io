I have a Masters degree in Computational and systems biology from KTH Royal Institute of Technology in Stockholm and a bachelors in Electronics and Communication engineering from JNTU college of Engineering, Kakinada. I worked for a few years as a software developer at Intergraph in the SG&I division.

Coming from an engineering background, I took additional courses on molecular biology, oncology and worked on projects at Karolinska institute to get a better understanding of biological research. I worked at D-BSSE, ETHZ in Basel on my master thesis (and published paper) to study digit patterning during limb development in the lab of Dagmar Iber.

My current area of research is the study of growth and mechanics during the development of the wing disc in Drosophila. I learned experimental genetics and microscopy techniques during the PhD to complement my computational modelling approaches. I am interested in understanding the mechanisms by which a wing is built from the bottom up. For my research, I employ a wide variety of techniques including experimental Drosophila genetics, various microscopy technqiues (Confocal, Electron, 2 photon, SPIM and STED), Image analysis and computational modelling.

This website is for my research interests, ideas and projects.

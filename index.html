About

I am a data scientist passionate about solving complex problems. After a career start in software product development, I decided to apply my problem-solving skills to the field of systems biology and resumed my academic studies. I received my masters from the Royal Institute of Technology in Stockholm (Sweden) and my PhD from the University of Zurich (Switzerland). I am trained in the wetlab, software development, and microscopy methods. I developed several methods for understanding underlying structures and patterns in biology with an engineering approach. During the past decade, I have worked within different teams in 5 countries to deliver top quality research. Currently, I am in a multi-disciplinary research project where I leverage expertise from the Wyss Institute at Harvard Medical School and the Paul Allen Discovery Center at the Tufts University to develop assays for understanding long distance signalling.
